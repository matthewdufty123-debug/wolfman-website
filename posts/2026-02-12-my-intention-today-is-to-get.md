## Today's Intention

My intention today is to get one task done at work. Only one. A really important one. Get it done, then I am not carrying anything into the weekend. This will make Love Day a whole lot more Lovely.

And with that I would like to venture into the 'Back to Work in the Office' debate that seems to be kicking off across the UK. What are my thoughts on this. I am personally a fan of the office so I'm not to fussed about the concept coming in more, however... The idea of full time office working for certain job roles is flawed. For me personally I can not be sure when ideas will strike and motivation will be high. So I like the flexibility to do my job from home and in unsavoury hours on these moments.

Last night I was working till midnight. It felt great, ideas were flowing, I achieved loads, my watch was telling me that my body was in a deep state of relaxation. If I were forced to be in the office, that moment of inspiration would have been missed and the company would be worse off today. Instead the company has benefited from giving me the flexibility to work like this. I currently have bit of a choice of how I structure my week. I can cluster meetings to days when I'm in the office, so we can having amazing face to face sessions. And I can plan deep thoughtful work for my home working days, where I can find my flow state without distraction.

So, my predictions on where this is going to go. The 'Back To the Office' thing is going to be a short lived theme/fad of 2026 and 2027. It will tail off in 2028 because our road and rail infrastructure does not cater for long distance travels to work, people will leave there roles for more local jobs. Talent will be constrained to geographical location and in two years time executives will start saying;

 "I need to employee a person of X skillset to get this job done. Let them work remotely, because I just need this job doing right".

Then 'The Great Talent Grab' will commence. once one big company starts hiring for hybrid workers, they all will.

The maths is simple for this scenario. You will have a far great pool of talent apply for a role when you open it up to the whole nation when compared to your local town or city. Unless, we had bullet trains. And trust me... I really really wish the UK would have bullet trains. It's something I fantasise about multiple times per month.

## I'm Grateful For

3 days a week in the office while it lasts. Then, I suppose more days in through 2027 until people change there mind and we revert back in 2028 to more hybrid working again.

And... To be clear. I'm not moaning about coming to the office. I really enjoy the office. The people vibe is nice fuel for life. Just dont want to have to move house every time I get a new job.

## I'm Grateful For

Waking up early. I am a believer that the best ideas in life are manifest first thing in the morning. Like more bullet trains. Why dont we connect all cities with bullet trains? Please?
