## Today's Intention

Poorly Matthew. Oh so poorly. It hit me Monday, the disease manifesting in my blood Infecting my soul. Still functional, but feeling like something was off. Then it hit hard. Tuesday the moaning started. So much moody and moaning. It was time to log off from work. Wednesday, the moans turned to groans. And today, the groans have become mild fussyness with some lingering snappy Matthew.

What is interesting about this illness curve is monitoring the thoughts in my head and the words that escape my mouth. For me, when i have a disease, the truth tends to find it's way to the surface. Some people call it moaning, and for me over the past three days there has been some of that. It's like the social filter we put on to protect ourselves is lifted and you get to hear a more true version of yourself.

I have had so many things come up for me the past few days. A part of me was like, "Gosh, give it rest." As I listened to my internal dialogue. But actually, it's infrequent ( we hope ) that we deal with disease. So take the opportunity to listen to the words that come up. There could be a really interesting insight in there. Something your subconscious has been sitting on, but did not have the courage to surface into your conscious being. Or, you might just be moaning because you have a sniffy nose. Either way it's fun to monitor. 

And so, my intention, is to be nice today. No more moaning. Let's bring a bit of love to the table and have a bit of fun.

## I'm Grateful For

A immune system that is able to work through disease. I love my immune system. Keeps me healthy so I can focus on my life goals.

## I'm Grateful For

Knowing when I have an opinion that is wrong or flawed. I generate ideas very quickly. Ones that can seem so convincing to me it can be hard to see any other way forward. But... I'm not always correct, and I have learnt to listen to colleagues critiques of my ideas. These critiques are critical. Ideas need to be challenged. Poked and prodded to see if they stand up. I only ever know what I know, and that might be enough to form an idea, but does the idea stand up when confronted with all things I don't know? The answer is, I don't know, so I welcome the challenge.

Ideas are amazing. They are so easy to dismiss, and I wonder how many great ideas have been generated over time, and squashed by some enabling body not giving them the time of day. So my advise. If you have an idea that you really REALLY, believe in. Don't let anyone dismiss it until it has been given the attention it deserves. Most times, the reason someone dismisses and idea is because they don't understand it. Only you truly understand it and it's your job to articulate it. But... Don't get lost and dismiss the criticism. This will be one of the greatest factors in its success.

Have a Great Day 🌻
